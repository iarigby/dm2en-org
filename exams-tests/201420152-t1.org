* Discrete mathematics 2. First test 2014-2015-2
  1. Draw a simple 8 vertex graphs with the following degrees or prove
     that such a graph doesn't exist:
     #+ATTR_LATEX: :environment inparaenum
     1) $6, 6, 6, 4, 3, 2, 2, 1$;
     2) $7, 6, 6, 6, 3, 2, 2, 0$;
     3) $6, 6, 4, 4, 3, 2, 2, 1$;
     4) $6, 6, 4, 4, 2, 2, 2, 1$;
  2. Show, that there is a closed directed Euler walk in a directed
     graph if $d^-(v)=2=d^+(v)$ for every vertex $v$.
  3. Show that any tree with at least two vertices is bipartite.
  4. Let $n=14$. Show that $(\mathbb{Z}_{n}^*,\cdot)$ is a group where
     $\mathbb{Z}_{n}^*=\{\overline{k}\in\mathbb{Z}_{n}:(k,n)=1\}$ \ie
     the residue classes relative prime to $n$ with multiplication.
  5. Show that $c\mapsto
     (\begin{smallmatrix}1&c\\0&1\end{smallmatrix})$ is a homomorphism
     from $(\mathbb{R},+)$ to $(\{(\begin{smallmatrix} 1&c\\0&1
     \end{smallmatrix}):c\in\mathbb{R}\},\cdot)$.
  6. In the dihedral group $D_n$ for $n=18$ and the elements of the
     group $g_1=\tau \circ \varepsilon^4 \circ \tau^5$, $g_2=\tau^{25}
     \circ \varepsilon^{21}$ answer the following questions:
     #+ATTR_LATEX: :environment inparaenum
     1) What is the order of $D_n$?
     2) Simplify $g_1$, $g_2$ and $g_1\circ g_2$.
     3) What are the sub\-group generated by $g_1$, $g_2$ and
        $g_1\circ g_2$?
     4) What is the order of $g_1$, $g_2$ and $g_1\circ g_2$?

  6. 
     1) $\lvert D_n \rvert = 2n = 36$.
     2) $g_1 = \varepsilon^{14}$; $g_2 = \tau \circ \varepsilon^3 =
        \varepsilon^{15} \circ \tau$, $g_1 \circ g_2 =
        \varepsilon^{14} \circ \varepsilon^{15} \circ \tau =
        \varepsilon^{11} \circ \tau$;
     3) $\langle g_1 \rangle = \{ e = \varepsilon^0, \varepsilon^2,
        \varepsilon^4, \ldots, \varepsilon^{16} \}$; $\langle g_2
        \rangle = \{ e, \varepsilon^{15} \circ \tau \}$; $\langle g_1
        \circ g_2 \rangle = \{ e, \varepsilon^{11} \circ \tau \}$;
     4) $\lvert g_1 \rvert = 9$; $\lvert g_1 \rvert = 2$; $\lvert g_1
        \circ g_2 \rvert = 2$.
